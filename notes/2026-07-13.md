## What surprised us

- **All three official `lerobot/*_pusht` Hub checkpoints predate the current
  processor-pipeline checkpoint format.** `lerobot-eval` against
  `lerobot/diffusion_pusht` failed with a raw 404 trying to fetch
  `policy_preprocessor.json` — a file that checkpoint never shipped, because
  it was uploaded before LeRobot introduced the `PolicyProcessorPipeline`
  format for normalization/pre/post-processing. LeRobot actually has a
  `ProcessorMigrationError` designed to catch exactly this and print the fix
  command, but the detection (`_should_suggest_migration` in
  `lerobot/processor/pipeline.py:1261`) only runs for **local directories** —
  for a bare Hub repo ID it just lets the bare `FileNotFoundError` propagate,
  so the error message gives no hint about what's actually wrong or how to
  fix it.
  - Fix: downloaded the checkpoint locally (`snapshot_download`), ran
    `python -m lerobot.processor.migrate_policy_normalization
    --pretrained-path <local_dir>`, pointed `--policy.path` at the migrated
    copy. Worked cleanly — this is the library's own sanctioned path, just
    not triggered automatically for Hub IDs.
  - This is content for write-up #1: the entire public `lerobot` org's PushT
    checkpoint zoo is currently broken against the pinned `lerobot==0.6.0`
    eval CLI out of the box.
- Real inference time was ~16 minutes for 5 episodes — nowhere near the
  "well under a minute" estimate from the Session 2 candidate proposal. The
  mechanism (denoising requires dozens of forward passes per action chunk,
  not one) is in the lesson note linked above. **Correction after reading
  `config.json`: not dozens — exactly 100** (`num_inference_steps` is `null`
  in this checkpoint, which falls back to `num_train_timesteps=100` at
  `modeling_diffusion.py:227-230`). Every chunk regeneration is 100 full
  U-Net forward passes, not an approximate "dozens."

## Inference loop, narrated against actual code

Traced through `lerobot==0.6.0`'s installed source rather than assuming from
memory. Full trace:

- `rollout()` — `lerobot/scripts/lerobot_eval.py:158-400` — the batched
  rollout loop that drove our eval. Per step: `preprocess_observation()`
  (`lerobot/envs/utils.py:68`) converts the env's raw numpy dict to LeRobot's
  tensor convention, then the observation passes through `env_preprocessor`
  and `preprocessor` (the policy's `PolicyProcessorPipeline` — the exact
  normalizer whose config file was missing on the stock Hub checkpoint) before
  `policy.select_action(observation)` runs under `torch.inference_mode()`.
- `DiffusionPolicy.select_action` — `modeling_diffusion.py:124-160` — holds
  two deques (built in `reset()`, line 91): an observation-history queue
  (`maxlen=n_obs_steps=2`) and a pending-action queue (`maxlen=n_action_steps=8`).
  When the action queue is empty, `predict_action_chunk` →
  `DiffusionModel.generate_actions` (line 307) → `conditional_sample` (line
  233) runs: start from `torch.randn(batch, horizon=16, action_dim=2)` (pure
  noise), then `for t in self.noise_scheduler.timesteps:` (line 257) — 100
  iterations — each calling `self.unet(sample, t, global_cond=...)` and one
  DDPM denoising step. `global_cond` (`_prepare_global_conditioning`, line
  269) is where the 2-step image history gets encoded by a ResNet18
  (`DiffusionRgbEncoder`) and concatenated with 2-step state history — the
  signal steering all 100 denoising steps.
- Of the 16 generated actions, only 8 (`n_action_steps`) are ever popped and
  executed via `.popleft()` before the queue empties and a fresh 100-step
  generation triggers. **16 predicted, 8 executed, 100 forward passes to get
  them** — concretely, not approximately.
- Env's declared rate is `fps=10` (100ms/step nominal), but our measured rate
  was ~640ms/step (16min ÷ ~300 steps) — over 6x slower. Nothing in the stock
  eval script separates *why*: sim physics cost, policy inference cost, and
  pre/post-processing cost are all folded into one number.

## Everything we could not see

The stock `lerobot-eval` output — aggregate reward/success numbers, an
`eval_info.json`, and per-episode MP4s — cannot answer any of the following.
This list seeds the observability work in later modules:

1. **Per-step inference latency.** We know 5 episodes took ~16 minutes total.
   We do not know how long any single `select_action` call took, or how that
   varied step to step.
2. **Chunk boundaries.** No record of *when* each 100-step denoising
   generation was triggered (queue-empty events) versus which of the 300
   env steps consumed from a given chunk. We inferred "16 generated, 8
   executed" from reading the source, not from anything the run itself
   logged.
3. **Action staleness.** Within a chunk, action index 7 (the last of the 8
   executed) was generated from an observation that's now several
   environment steps old. We have no measurement of how stale any executed
   action was relative to the observation available at the moment it
   actually ran.
4. **Denoising-step-level cost.** Is the 100-step reverse loop uniform in
   per-step cost, or do certain steps (device transfer, scheduler math)
   dominate? No per-step timing inside `conditional_sample` exists anywhere.
5. **Raw per-step state.** No persisted record of the agent's (x,y) position
   or the T-block's pose at each step outside of decoding the rendered MP4
   frame-by-frame. Can't correlate a specific action to a specific numeric
   observation after the fact.
6. **Discarded predictions.** Each chunk generates 16 actions but only 8 are
   used; the other 8 (what the policy "would have done" further into the
   future) are silently dropped and never inspectable.
7. **Why episode 1 failed.** We have `reward=0.0` and a video. No per-step
   reward curve, no record of the block's pose over time, no way to say
   *when* or *why* it diverged without frame-by-frame visual inspection.
8. **Compute breakdown.** No CPU/MPS utilization, memory, or thermal data.
   Unclear whether the ~640ms/step is inference-bound, sim-bound, or
   contention with something else running on the machine.
9. **Sim vs. policy time split.** `env.step()` (pymunk physics) and
   `policy.select_action()` (100 U-Net passes) are two very different costs
   folded into one wall-clock number per step. No breakdown exists.
10. **Pre/post-processing overhead.** Normalization, image crop/resize —
    folded into the same undifferentiated per-step time as everything else.
11. **Per-episode variance.** `eval_info.json` gives `eval_s` (total) and
    `eval_ep_s` (average) — no per-episode latency breakdown, so we can't
    tell if episode 1 (the failure) was also anomalous in timing.
12. **Statistical confidence.** n=5 carries no variance/confidence
    information — 80% could plausibly be 60% or 100% at this sample size.
